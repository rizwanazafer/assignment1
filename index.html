Residents advised to be cautious about price gouging following Hurricane Ida

By Rizwana Zafer
Oct. 15, 2021

During a virtual town hall meeting, Queens Borough President Donovan Richards urged residents to be cautious of price gouging following Hurricane Ida. Following Hurricane Sandy, a few individuals and companies took advantage of the natural disaster by overcharging residents for construction repair costs, which is against NYS law. Richards has advised residents to be wary of similar price gouging tactics following Hurricane Ida as well. He has advised homeowners to take a number of precautions before hiring an individual and/or business.

What Qualifies as Flood Damage?

In order to qualify for assistance, individuals must provide proof of flood damage caused by Hurricane Ida. These include:

  • Flooding in the home
  • Sewer backups
  • Flood-damaged items such as carpet, flooring, curtains, etc.
  • Each case is unique, so individuals are encouraged to document as much as possible in order to provide adequate proof.

Apply for assistance through the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)

Richards urged residents to apply for FEMA assistance immediately. Assistance is available for homeowners, qualified undocumented persons, and cases in which a car/business was damaged. Individuals can apply for FEMA assistance by submitting a completed application through the official FEMA website, and the deadline to apply is Nov. 5th.

Check for licenses and ask for references

Richards recommended that residents ask individuals and businesses hired for repairs for their official licenses. Contractors are required by law to have liability insurance, which residents should also ask for proof of. As an extra precautionary measure, Richards suggested that residents should ask for multiple references over the phone.
